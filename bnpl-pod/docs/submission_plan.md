# Submission plan — SSRN → NBER → JFS

**Target primary venue:** *Journal of Financial Stability* (JFS)
**Working-paper runway:** SSRN → NBER
**Owner:** Siddharth Verma (UIUC, FIN 580, Spring 2026 cohort)

---

## Why JFS is the lead

1. **Scope fit.** JFS publishes work on measurement of systemic and
   sub-systemic credit stress, construct validity of new financial-
   stability sensors, and the off-ledger segment of household credit.
   The v2 paper — a behavioral-sensor construct-validity paper for
   an off-bureau consumer-credit category — is squarely in scope.

2. **Format fit.** JFS accepts 30–50 page methodology papers with
   strong disclosure and pre-registration discipline. The v2 paper,
   with §2.5 construct-validity decomposition + §6 falsification
   gauntlet + §10 threats-to-validity, fits the JFS structural
   template better than RFS or JF.

3. **Referee culture.** JFS referees emphasise measurement rigor over
   headline economic magnitude. This is exactly the audience for
   whom the sealed alt abstract's narrow framing plays well.

4. **Precedent.** deHaan-Kim-Lourie-Zhu 2024 JFE and Guttman-Kenney-
   Firth-Gathergood 2023 (JCR) are the two closest in-kind papers;
   both are measurement-first treatments of consumer-credit
   categories that would have been at home in JFS had the authors
   preferred it.

## Runway

### Stage 1 — SSRN working paper (week 0)

**Trigger:** v2 paper PDF builds cleanly with all retrospective,
roadmap, and sealed-abstract docs linked on title page footnote.

**Steps:**
1. Register SSRN author ID if not already (Siddharth Verma, UIUC).
2. Upload v2 PDF to SSRN under the "Financial Economics Network"
   and "Banking & Financial Institutions Network" sub-pages.
3. Cross-list to "Household Finance," "Credit Risk," and "Behavioral
   Finance" sub-networks.
4. Abstract on SSRN mirrors paper abstract verbatim (≤ 250 words).
5. Announce on personal research page + one LinkedIn post linking
   the SSRN page. **Do not tweet the +925 bp number.** Lead with
   the construct-validity framing.

**Purpose:** establish a time-stamped public record of the work
that predates any journal submission. If a referee later claims
novelty dispute, the SSRN timestamp is the evidence.

### Stage 2 — NBER working paper (week 2–4)

**Trigger:** ≥ 100 SSRN downloads *or* 2 weeks post-SSRN, whichever
comes first.

**Method:** submission via affiliated UIUC finance faculty. NBER
accepts only through an NBER-member sponsor. Likely sponsor:
identify a UIUC Finance faculty member on the NBER Corporate
Finance or Household Finance program and request sponsorship.

**Purpose:** NBER circulation reaches journal editors and the
institutional-research community (Fed, Treasury, BIS, IMF) faster
than SSRN alone. Also raises desk-reject bar at JFS.

### Stage 3 — JFS submission (week 8–12)

**Pre-submission checklist:**
- [ ] 4 weeks of SSRN circulation minimum
- [ ] At least 2 comments from any readers, incorporated
- [ ] Cover letter drafted highlighting: (a) novel measurement
      contribution, (b) pre-registered falsification gauntlet,
      (c) explicit scope limits (no tradability claim)
- [ ] Suggested referees list (5 names) prepared
- [ ] `docs/v1_retrospective.md` and `docs/alt_abstract_sealed.md`
      available as online supplement
- [ ] Code and data availability statement drafted, pointing to
      GitHub (`github.com/vermasidd1502/bnpl-trap`)
- [ ] Author responsibility statement drafted per UIUC policy

**Submission:** via JFS online system (Elsevier EVISE or equivalent).
Initial decision expected in 8–16 weeks.

### Stage 4 — Revision cycles

Plan for **2 revision cycles** before either acceptance or redirect.
If JFS rejects on fit, redirect to:
- **Primary backup:** Journal of Banking & Finance (JBF) — broader
  scope, accepts the methodology papers JFS might reject on
  "too narrow."
- **Secondary backup:** Journal of Financial Econometrics (JFE,
  the Oxford one, not the Bhattacharyya-Hirshleifer JFE) — pure-
  methodology backup if the policy framing is the rejection reason.
- **Tertiary:** Review of Financial Studies (RFS) — less likely
  given single-category focus, but the construct-validity
  methodology is RFS-calibre.

### Stage 5 — Post-acceptance

- Code/data DOI via Zenodo snapshot of the GitHub repo at
  acceptance commit SHA.
- Author's version on personal page + SSRN (replacing the
  working-paper PDF).
- One-page non-technical summary for a finance-industry blog
  outlet (e.g., Alphaville, ProMarket) — under the narrow
  construct-validity framing, **not the Subprime 2.0 framing.**

## What NOT to do

1. **Do not submit to RFS or JF as the lead venue.** Both would
   likely desk-reject on single-category scope, and a desk-reject
   at a top-3 venue delays JFS submission by the editorial
   turnaround time. Save the top-3 shots for a v3 paper with
   multi-category generalisation.

2. **Do not cross-submit.** Double-submission is an ethics
   violation. If JFS takes >16 weeks for initial decision,
   withdraw in writing and redirect to JBF.

3. **Do not lead any announcement with the +925 bp figure.** The
   v1 retrospective retracted it. Any announcement that leads
   with the bp number undoes the retrospective's credibility.

4. **Do not cite v1 as the published version.** Cite the v2 SSRN
   paper with the SSRN ID as the canonical reference.
